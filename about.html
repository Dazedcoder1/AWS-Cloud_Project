What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services‚Äîincluding servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence‚Äîover the Internet ("the cloud") to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.

Instead of owning their own computing infrastructure or data centers, companies can rent access to anything from applications to storage from a cloud service provider. This revolutionary approach allows businesses to focus on their core operations while leveraging the power of scalable, on-demand computing resources.

Key Characteristics

Three fundamental pillars that define cloud computing

Elasticity

Cloud resources can automatically scale up or down in real-time based on demand. Whether you need more processing power during peak hours or want to reduce resources during slow periods, elasticity ensures optimal performance and cost efficiency without manual intervention.

Scalability

Effortlessly expand or contract your infrastructure to match your business growth. Start small with minimal resources and seamlessly scale to enterprise-level capacity as your needs evolve. Scalability ensures your cloud infrastructure grows with you.

Pay-as-You-Go

Only pay for the computing resources you actually use, when you use them. No upfront capital investment, no wasted resources, no long-term commitments. This flexible pricing model transforms IT costs from fixed capital expenses to variable operational expenses.
